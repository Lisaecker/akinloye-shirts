The Story

Two Worlds.
One Shirt.

When I flew to Nigeria for the funeral of Akin's grandfather, the tailor arrived at the house in the middle of the grief. She opened her notebook, took measurements — and a few days later, at the funeral celebration — which in Nigeria is always also a celebration of life — the fabrics carried meaning. Who belonged to the closest family, who to the extended.

"In that moment I understood: clothing here is a language."

I'm Lisa, born in Munich. My husband Akin, who grew up in Oyo, works today in a corporate environment in Munich, and wears what everyone there wears: the international business look. Every family visit, we bring Ankara prints back home — vivid, full of meaning. In Munich, they sit without a place. Not forgotten. Just without a home.

"What if Akin's business look did what clothing in Nigeria has done for generations?"

That question became AKINLOYE. A premium shirt in European tailoring. At the cuffs and collar lining: handpicked Ankara fabric. Meaningful. Unmistakable.

Lisa Founder, AKINLOYE

without either one giving up the other."
